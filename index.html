About

The Engineer
Behind the Numbers

I grew up fascinated by the physics of flight — not the glamour of aircraft, but the forces underneath. What keeps a rocket from tearing itself apart at max-q? Why does a bell nozzle lose efficiency at altitude? These questions pulled me into aerospace engineering, and they still drive everything I do.

At SASTRA University, I used a supersonic blow-down wind tunnel and Schlieren imaging to study chevron nozzle shock-cell structure — achieving a 5% reduction in shock intensity through geometry refinement. That project taught me the lesson I carry into every program: simulation tells you where to look. Hardware tells you what's actually happening.

"Not a simulation study. An actual engine, burning actual propellant, validated against thrust targets."

In 15 months at Prime Toolings, I led three full design-to-test propulsion programs — including India's first liquid-fueled aerospike engine. I don't just run solvers. I own the boundary conditions, defend the numbers, and show up at static fire.

Open to any domain where the engineering is serious and the hardware is real.
